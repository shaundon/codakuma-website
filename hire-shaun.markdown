# Hire me

<p class="hire-tagline">Hand-baked iOS. Small-batch React Native.<br>Mobile engineering for teams who care about the details.</p>

I find the issues your mobile team doesn't know they have, fix them quietly, and bring the team along so the improvements stick.

## Who I am

I'm Shaun. I've spent over a decade building software, mostly on mobile and design systems — at [Skyscanner](https://skyscanner.net), [Duffel](https://duffel.com), [Packfleet](https://packfleet.com), and most recently [Light](https://light.inc). The [longer version is on my CV](/cv/).

On the side [I make iOS apps](/apps). They've been downloaded over 500,000 times and are regularly featured by Apple as examples of best practice.

## What I do

- **Mobile audits** — finding the issues your team doesn't realise are slowing them down
- **Build, release & CI** — local builds, OTA updates, GitHub Actions, build flavours, sub-minute CI
- **Codebase modernisation** — major version upgrades, dependency cleanup, lint and PR hygiene
- **Accessibility** — VoiceOver, Dynamic Type, reduce motion and transparency, audits and fixes
- **Component libraries & testing culture** — reusable primitives, kitchen-sink screens, unit tests
- **Knowledge transfer** — your devs become advocates for best practice, not dependent on me

## How I work

- Async-first. Calls when they're useful, otherwise text.
- Scoped by outcome, not by hours-on-team.
- I write code. The deliverable is the working software, not a slide deck.
- I bring people along. Your devs end up more capable, not more dependent on me.

## Two ways to work together

### A short engagement

Two or three weeks, fixed scope and price. I come in, identify the highest-impact issues, fix as many as I can in the time, and leave you with a written summary and a prioritised list of what's left.

Best if you've got a hunch your app could be in better shape but can't quite name what's wrong.

### Something longer

A few months, working alongside your team async. Continuous improvement, knowledge transfer, the lot. The team gets faster as I'm there, and stays faster after I leave.

Best if there's real work to do and you want me to bring people along as I go.

## What I've shipped recently

A few highlights from a recent engagement, where I came in to clean up a React Native app that had grown out of a proof-of-concept:

- Cut CI from over two minutes to well under one
- Untangled a stuck Expo upgrade and moved the team forwards two major versions
- Switched to continuous native generation, removed checked-in `ios` and `android` folders
- Set up over-the-air updates and automated GitHub-Actions-driven releases
- Built a small library of accessible, themeable components and a kitchen-sink screen to test them
- Ran an end-to-end accessibility pass — VoiceOver, Dynamic Type, reduce motion, reduce transparency
- Replaced a long-running development branch with `main`, added type and lint checks to CI
- Wrote the docs and scripts so a new dev can clone, install, and run the app in minutes

> The proof: a new developer joined the team, got the app running, and shipped a release on her first day without asking for help once.
